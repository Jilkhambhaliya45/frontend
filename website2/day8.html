Day 8

  • Devi MahaGauri

Devi Maha Gauri is one of the manifestations of the Hindu goddess Durga and is revered during the nine-day festival of Navaratri. She is the eighth form of Devi Durga and is associated with purity, serenity, and divine grace. Her name "Maha Gauri" can be translated to mean "extremely white" or "purest of the pure," signifying her pristine and untainted nature. Here are some key details about Devi Maha Gauri: Appearance: Devi Maha Gauri is typically depicted as a young and radiant goddess with a fair complexion. She is often portrayed wearing white or green attire, which symbolizes her purity and connection to nature. In her four arms, she holds a trident (trishul) and a drum (damaru), while her other two hands are in mudras symbolizing blessings and fearlessness.



  • Goddess of the day : Maa Mahagauri
  • Color of the Day : Sky Blue
  • Flower of the Day : Arabian Jasmine or Mogra
  • Sweet / Prasadam of the Day : Coconut
